Risotto

Description

  • TIME
  • SERVINGS
  • DIFFICULTY
  • 25 MINUTES
  • 2
  • LEVEL 3

Risotto, originally an Italian porridge dish, has been around longer than even Graham or Cranston, hard though that may be to believe. Made from just a few simple ingredients, risotto was long viewed as a poor man's supper. And yet it is a true delicacy when prepared correctly! But preparing it correctly is the tricky part. On your island, you won't have any trouble if you get the recipe from Nook's Cranny or the Café. And in the real world, this recipe makes preparing risotto a snap!

Ingredients

For the basil pesto

  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 pinch salt
  • 2 bunches fresh basil (about 1 ounce), plus more for garnish
  • 2 ounces grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for garnish
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Pinch ground black pepper

For the risotto

  • 3 1/2 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3/4 cup arborio rice
  • Scant 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1 3/4 cups vegetable broth
  • Pinch salt
  • Pinch ground black pepper

Also required:

  • Mortar and pestle

Steps

To make the basil pesto:

  1. Briefly toast the pine nuts in a small dry pan over medium heat. Remove from the heat and allow them to cool slightly.
  2. Crush the pine nuts and garlic in a mortar until you have a smooth paste. (Alternatively, process with a food processor.) Add the salt and basil and crush thoroughly until a green mass forms. Now add the Parmesan and oil and stir or cursh again thoroughly until a creamy paste forms. Season to taste with black pepper and possibly a bit more salt.

To make the risotto:

  1. In a large saucepan over medium heat, melt 1 1/2 tablespoons of the butter. Add the onions and sweat until translucent, about 2 to 3 minutes. Slowly stir in the rice and allow it to briedfly absorb the butter, then deglaze the pan with wine. Increase the heat to medium-high and simmer until the rice absorbs the wine, about 3 minutes. Then gradually stir in the vegetable broth over medium heat. Season to taste with salt and black pepper and cook, stirring constantly, for about 15 minutes. Shortly before the end of the cooking time, stir in the remainin 2 tablespoons of butter so the risotto gets nice and creamy.
  2. Transfer the cooked risotto to plates, top with basil pesto to taste, sprinkle with a bit of freshly grated Parmesan, and serve garnished with basil.
